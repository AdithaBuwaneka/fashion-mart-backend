Design Approval Criteria

Quality Assessment Standards

  • Technical Quality: Image resolution, format compliance, color accuracy
  • Design Originality: Uniqueness and creative value assessment
  • Market Viability: Commercial potential and target market fit
  • Brand Alignment: Consistency with platform brand guidelines
  • Production Feasibility: Manufacturing complexity and cost analysis
  • Legal Compliance: Copyright, trademark, and legal clearance
  • Safety Standards: Product safety and regulatory compliance
  • Customer Appeal: Market research and customer preference alignment

Approval Decision Matrix

  • Immediate Approval: Exceeds quality standards, high market potential
  • Conditional Approval: Good quality with minor modifications needed
  • Revision Required: Potential but needs significant improvements
  • Rejection: Does not meet minimum quality or legal standards
  • Hold for Review: Requires additional assessment or market research

Performance Metrics & KPIs

Inventory Management KPIs

  • Inventory Turnover: 6-8x annually target
  • Stock-out Rate: <2% target for popular items
  • Carrying Cost: <25% of inventory value
  • Order Fill Rate: >98% customer order fulfillment
  • Inventory Accuracy: >99.5% system vs. physical count
  • Gross Margin: Product profitability tracking
  • Days in Inventory: Average days stock on hand
  • Forecast Accuracy: Demand prediction precision

Design Approval Metrics

  • Approval Rate: Percentage of designs approved
  • Review Time: Average time to review submissions
  • Designer Satisfaction: Feedback quality ratings
  • Market Success: Approved design sales performance
  • Quality Improvement: Designer submission quality trends

Emergency Procedures

Critical Stock Situations

  • Stock-out Crisis: Emergency supplier contact and expedited ordering
  • Quality Issues: Immediate product recall and supplier notification
  • System Failures: Manual tracking and backup procedures
  • Demand Spikes: Rapid restock and capacity expansion
  • Supplier Issues: Alternative supplier activation

Communication Protocols

  • Internal Teams: Immediate notification of critical issues
  • Management: Escalation procedures for major problems
  • Suppliers: Direct communication channels for urgent needs
  • Customers: Transparent communication about stock availability
